VI. Past Perfect

December 3, 2019

When we talk about and action that happened in the past but also we need to show that this action develops in the past before the recent past we can use the past perfect. This definition could be a little confused but we can see better with an example:(example1)

  • When John came to home, I had eaten the dinner.

The estructure of this verbal tense is the following:

Subject + had + past participle of the main verb.

Be carefull!

When the time in the sentence is clear, it is usually wrong to use the past perfect tense.

This mean something like this:

  • Jane and I was in the shoppingmall last week when she fed in the stairs of the mall. All of the people in the surroundings come and help Jane while I call at emergency.

Look! Since the beginning, we have known that the actions develop in the past, so in this case, it is not necessary to use the structure of the past perfect

Pay attention: We use this verbal tense when we need to express an action that happen first that other action in the past (example 1).
