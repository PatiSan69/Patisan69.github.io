VIII. Modals verbs

December 5, 2019

Modals verbs we can use when we need to express ability, possibility, advice or imposibility. This type of verbs are like helping verbs in a sentence because they are always accompanied by the main verb.

When we use these type of verb the main verb mainteing their original form:

  • She can go to the pool.

  • My father was able to run 5 milles everyday in the morning.

Look!!! the main verb of the sentence maintein their form. With this type of verb the main verb never change this make all more easy!!!

Types of modals verbs.

These are the most commun modals:

  • Can: express possibility

  • Could: Have the same meaning that the verb can but this make reference a past time or an actions or events that happens in a time before now. Also express alternatives.

  • May: This modal verb is formal, express possibility like as can.

  • Might: This modal verb mean suggestions or also can express possibility.

  • Will: The modal verb will make reference a time in a future. express that something is planning to happen in th future.

  • Would: Make reference a habits in a past time or also express in future the conditions for something happen.

  • Must: Express needs and sugestions.

  • Should: This verb is use for express advice, opinions and actions, sugestions.

Let’s look at some examples with modals verbs:

I can wake up at 5:oo am everyday

You must study hard for the task

You must put up the price in the hotel

You should end up your homework

Lauren is a good song and has a lot of examples of modals verbs. This song have a lot of modals verbs pay attention.

In English, we have another type of modals verbs and these are known like false modals verbs. False modals verbs all have to and this makes that they change depending on the subject in the sentence, also they call false because they change her form this depends of the subject in the sentences. Look this examples:

  • She have to run

but the correct form is this:

  • She has to run.

In this list of verbs we have: have to, be able to, ought to,have got to.

Exist one exception, and this is: ought to. (in meaning is the same that should)

This phrasal verb not change!.

  • You ought to go to school now
