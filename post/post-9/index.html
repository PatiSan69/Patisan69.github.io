IX. Gerunds and Infinitives

December 9, 2019

In this moment you should be able to know what means the verb plus ing. you can say that is the form of the verb present participle but also we can say that is a gerund.

Gerunds are verbs plus ing. the differences between the other topic that we develop is that in this case (In present perfect continuous we use the present participle of the verb, this mean verb plus “ING”), they not acting like verbs in a sentences actually, they can act as nouns, subject or the object in a sentences.

  • We have a dinner party with friends and enjoying all night

or

  • Planning party in house of Alfredo

On other hand, we have infinitives. Infinitives is to plus main verb as to drink, to see, to believe. They look like base verbs in sentences, also they not are the main verb and with him is not necessary a tense.

  • I want to believe in the system of justice of my country but is hard.

If you have wondering about an example about this, we have in this song of the beatles Strawbery Fields Forever a good example of gerunds. Living is easy with eyes closed, Living is a gerund and we can to see that is used as the subject of the sentence.

But wait!!! In this point you maybe have a lot of questions about their use.

Another thing is the use of gerund after prepositions.

  • Lisa is good at dancing.

We must remember that with after a gerund it necessary a proposition before them.

With infinitive is useful remember that we can use this after the verb and the object combination in a sentence.

  • You can see that we must stay for 4 hours to agree with the company

Lastly, in the last example we can see the use of gerunds and infinitives after verbs, but we must be careful be the use of them as the subject in a sentence.

  • Dancing is my favorite form of exercise

But how is with infinitives as subject of a sentence??

  • To leave now is foolish (formal)

This example accomplish with the grammar rule but sounds weird because is very formal, so because of that we can say:

  • It is foolish to leave now.

Gerund and infinitives are a tricky point in English grammar so, I recommend read and practice!!!
