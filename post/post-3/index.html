III. Stative Verbs

December 2, 2019

In English, if you want to express and state, thoughts, emotions, or senses we can use stative verbs. This verbs like the title said, express states. It is important pay attention!!! this type of verbs cannot used for describe actions, or in continuous form (continuous is a verbal tense, that we have discussed more them (Ing)).

We can categorize this verbs

  • Thoughts: Believe, agree, remember, understand, prefer, know.

  • Emotions: Love, hates, like, dislike.

  • Senses: Tastes, hear, smell, sound, see, feel, look.

  • States of being: Has, bee, appear, need, want.

Look this examples!!!!

  • I believe in law.

  • I prefer run in the morning that in the afternoon.

  • I bee read a research about the food.

  • I love read looking at the sunset.

We can have some exceptions. We can express actions with stative verbs, but these depends of the context and the sentences.

Let´s see the following example

  • I have a old car (Possesion-state)

  • I´m having a little snack (action)

  • I’m thinking about the rules in English grammar.

  • I was think that is not a good idea (state-opinion).

  • I was seeing the movie last week.

  • I see that is not good.
