XIV. Pronouns

December 10, 2019

Sometimes in a text, we need to replace a noun with other word because sometimes could be repetitive the use of the same noun in the same sentence. For example, this could be one situation that can explain this topic: “Maria is the best student of the class but he doesn´t know anything about sport and because of that her have the worst qualification of all the class”. Look like we refer Maria in two occasion with “Her”, her is a pronoun.

Exist different types of pronouns. In this chapter we develop four of them: Possessive, personal, indifinite, reflexive.

This means that in a sentence we can use that pronoun like a subject, this pronoun take the place of specific nouns like name of a person,things or something like that.

When in a sentence we make reference about something but this something is not the subject of the sentence we can use pronoun but we have to use a object pronoun.

Soo, in a sentence we can have subject and object pronoun.

With personal pronouns like subject in a sentence we have:

  • I, you , she , he , it, we ,they

And object pronoun:

  • me, you, him, her it, us, them

With possesive adjective we have:

  • my, your, his, her, its, our, their, your.

And with possesive pronoun we have:

  • mine, yours, his, hers, its, ours, theirs.

finally, we have possesive or reflexive pronoun:

  • myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, themselves.

We can see with more order the different pronoun.

Personal Object Possesive adjective Possesive Pronoun Reflexive
