I. Present Perfect

December 1, 2019

When we need to talk about actions and events that start in the past without a specific time and that action in the present has relevance we can express these with the verbal tense known as present perfect.This is an example about the use of present perfect:

They have met in the classroom.

It is important remember that this verbal tense can not use when we need to express actions with specific time about when are happened this.

Incorrect: They have met in the clasroom this afternoon.

Another examples

  • I has studied English since 2015.

  • You has ridden a motorcycle for 5 years.

If you pay attention about the examples you can notice that we use a specific time in two sentences. We can make this with the use of since and for. With these, we can indicate an action that began in the past and still going now.

The structure for the present perfect is the following:

Has / have (not) + Past Participles.

But wait!!!! what you mean when you say past participles?!!

Well, we need to know the form of verbs in English. firstly we have three formS of the verbS in english and this are Simple form, Simple past and Past participle.

In english we have irregular and regular verbs.

With regular verbs, the simple past and past participle of the verb are the same things!!! But with irregular verbs, the simple past of the verb is different than the past participles. For example the verb keep is a irregular verb, so because of that, when we want use this verb and we describe and actions that require the use of past participles of this verb Kept, we have looked this form of the verb.

Look this example:

  • Since the last week I kept with the keys of Arturo .

It is important to consider that with past participles we always need auxiliar verb (Is like a helping verb) . With this type of verbs, we have list of them, that help us at beginning of the correct use of this verb.
