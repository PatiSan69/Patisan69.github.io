XIII. Phrasal verbs

December 10, 2019

In English, we have another type of verb and this are known as phrasal verbs. These groups of verbs generally have two words like this “ Get up”(compound noun). With intuition you can say that the meaning of this verb is something like this: "put something up", but is not, and because of that we need to be careful!.

Phrasal verbs have special meaning and for the person who is trying to learn English this could be a little tricky but with some practice, we can learn some of these verbs.

There are some example with this type of verb:

  1. I get up of the couch and walk to the door.

  2. I wake up at 7:00 am everyday.

  3. Monica fall down on the test and she was very sad but I said her that she should’t be sad because fall through isn’t bad.

  4. I give up with my uncle, she is a crazy person.

  5. We hope to break throught soon in the fight againts cancer.

With this type of verbs we have two types:

Transitive and intransitive

First, transitive verbs need “transfers” their actions to an object with intransitive a object is not necessary.

  • I bring coffee

Cofee is the object. If we said “I bring”. This is wrong because sentences are incomplete “you bring what??”.

With phrasal verbs transitive can separate in a sentence.

Like this:

  • I put hat on and go to the party.

Look!!! “put on” is the phrasal verbs and we can separate the verb put the object in the midle.

With intransitive verbs is not necessary an object. This type of verb have sense without and object.

  • I think.

With phrasal verbs intransitive that we cannot separate.

  • We are breaking down the barriers of the society.

So, How we can know if a verb is a transitive or intransitive??

For recognize when we use a transitive or intransitive verb you need to know everything said previously. If you express and action and you realize that with the verb is necessary and object so it is a transitive verb, in case that an object is not necessary it is an intransitive verb.

We need to be careful with the use of these verbs and this is because in ocasion a verb can be transitive or intransitive and in every form, they change their meaning. Let’s look an example with the phrasal verb “take off”:

Transitive: mean remove something.

Intransitive: to leave the ground and fly.

  • When the last ninja take off their suit. we going to see the real face of the justice

  • My friend Lisa is going to take a fly off diminic island at 5:00pm
